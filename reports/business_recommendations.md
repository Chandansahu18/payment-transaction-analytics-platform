## Executive Summary

**Period covered:** January 2024 - June 2025

**Prepared by:** Chandan Sahu

**Data sources:** Transaction generator, dbt warehouse marts, PowerBI dashboard

---

## HEADLINE FINDING

The platform recorded **₹3.4Bn GMV** across **500K transactions** (Jan 2024 - Jun 2025) at **92.6% success** and **3.5% fraud**, with estimated loss of **~₹88M** (2.6% of GMV). Risk is uneven - it clusters in odd-hour and high-risk category combinations, a small merchant subset, and 2,600+ velocity flags awaiting review.

---

## KEY FINDING 1 - Fraud above comfort band

Portfolio fraud at 3.5% exceeds the 2-3% band typical of mature payment networks. Loss exposure is manageable today but will scale with GMV if merchant and velocity SLAs weaken. Fraud clusters in specific patterns - targeted controls outperform network-wide declines.

## KEY FINDING 2 - Time & category concentration

Fraud uplift is highest in 1-5 AM and categories such as Travel and Electronics. Hour-category combinations drive disproportionate risk versus the portfolio average. Step-up authentication and category monitoring protect GMV better than friction on low-risk rails (UPI, Groceries).

## KEY FINDING 3 - Merchant risk is concentrated

Among volume-reliable merchants, fraud rates range 0.9%-11% (platform avg 3.5%). A minority exceed High Risk bands or the 5% review threshold and need partnership review. Surgical action on flagged merchants is sufficient - mass offboarding is not required.

## KEY FINDING 4 - Reactivation with lapse risk

RFM split: ~5.2K Active, ~2.6K Recent, ~1.8K Lapsed, ~2.3K Inactive. High-value lapsed customers offer a win-back opportunity, but return transactions need enhanced fraud screening. Short data history limits cohort trend depth; engagement opportunity remains actionable.

## KEY FINDING 5 - Velocity queue drives daily control

2,600+ transactions are flagged for action under current velocity rules. Critical cases are few; Medium-tier backlog is the operational norm. Queue clearance within 24 hours is the highest-ROI control - more effective than adding static rules.

---

## BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS

### 1. Clear the velocity queue every day

Review the top 20 highest-risk users and resolve flagged cases within 24 hours. The `velocity_anomaly_detection` mart already prioritises by `velocity_score` with `action_required` flags - this is a process change, not a platform change.

**Owner:** Fraud Operations | **Timeline:** Immediate

### 2. Act on high-risk merchants early

Merchants with reliable volume (`is_rate_reliable = TRUE`) in High Risk band or fraud rate above 5% get transaction limits or manual review within 7 days. ~12 seeded high-fraud merchants are visible on Page 04; automate escalation so none slip past the threshold.

**Owner:** Merchant Partnerships + Risk | **Timeline:** 7 days

### 3. Add step-up verification in risky windows

For Travel and Electronics payments above ₹10K between 1-5 AM, require OTP or biometric check. The fraud scoring engine already flags `fraud_risk_level = high` or `critical` - these classifications exist in the warehouse but are not fed back into the payment flow.

**Owner:** Risk Engineering | **Timeline:** 14 days

### 4. Win back high-value lapsed customers safely

Run campaigns only for High Monetary + Lapsed segment users; add enhanced fraud screening on the first two return payments. These users represent disproportionate revenue with zero fraud history - differentiated SLAs improve retention without increasing loss.

**Owner:** Growth + Risk | **Timeline:** 30 days

### 5. Publish a monthly risk report

Track fraud rate, fraud loss, queue backlog, and merchants above 5% fraud - use data to adjust rules, not one-off changes. The platform produces all required metrics via `marts` tables and `reporting.*` views; formalise the cadence.

**Owner:** Risk Analytics | **Timeline:** Monthly

---

## DATA LIMITATIONS

1. **Short history (Jan 2024 - Jun 2025).** Only 18 months of data - trend lines may look flat compared with 3-5 years of real production data.
2. **Synthetic data.** Built for pipeline demo; use insights for risk logic and segments, not exact loss forecasting.
3. **Single-platform view.** Does not track users across devices or wallets - real systems would link full customer identity.

---

## Methodology

**Data sources:** Synthetic Indian payment data - ~500,000 transactions (Jan 2024-Jun 2025), 12,000 users, 600 merchants. Generated by [`generator/transaction_generator.py`](../generator/transaction_generator.py).

**Pipeline:** Raw CSVs ingested into PostgreSQL (`raw` schema). Transformed through medallion architecture (`staging` -> `intermediate` -> `marts`) using dbt Core with 81 automated data quality tests (81/81 PASS). Fraud scoring engine in intermediate layer - composite score (0-100), 1h/24h velocity windows, human-readable `fraud_reason`. Analytics consumed via seven-page Power BI dashboard connected to `marts` tables + `reporting.*` views.

**Analytical methods used:** Fraud risk scoring (weighted signal model), velocity window analysis (1h/24h trailing counts), merchant risk profiling with `is_rate_reliable` guardrails, RFM-style customer segmentation (`fraud_customer_segments`), off-hours vs normal-hours fraud comparison, MoM GMV trending, and cohort retention analysis (warehouse depth only).
